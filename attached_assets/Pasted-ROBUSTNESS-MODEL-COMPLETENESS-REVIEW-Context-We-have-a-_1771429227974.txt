ROBUSTNESS & MODEL COMPLETENESS REVIEW

Context:
We have a UK divorce financial modelling engine producing scenario-based outputs.

Your task:
Conduct a structured review of model robustness and recommend enhancements or confirm completeness.

Do NOT add unnecessary complexity.
Focus on financial sustainability modelling integrity.

=================================================
PART 1 – TRANSFER & MAINTENANCE LOGIC REVIEW
=================================================

1) Confirm whether the model includes:
   - Spousal maintenance
   - Child maintenance (CMS)
   - One-time capital transfers
   - One-time transition costs

2) If not included, recommend minimal implementation that:
   - Adjusts monthly surplus
   - Impacts runway projection
   - Adjusts stability score where relevant

3) Confirm no double-counting occurs.

=================================================
PART 2 – DEBT ALLOCATION PRECISION
=================================================

1) Confirm whether debts can be:
   - Assigned to Party A
   - Assigned to Party B
   - Marked as joint

2) Confirm settlement logic correctly reallocates debt in retain scenarios.

3) Recommend improvements if debt allocation is simplistic.

=================================================
PART 3 – TRANSACTION COST COVERAGE
=================================================

1) Confirm selling costs logic.
2) Confirm whether:
   - Early repayment charges
   - Legal fees
   - Moving costs
   - Stamp duty
   are included or excluded.

3) If excluded, recommend whether:
   - Add “Additional One-Off Costs” field
   OR
   - Explicit exclusion statement

=================================================
PART 4 – RUNWAY & LIQUIDITY STRENGTHENING
=================================================

1) Confirm runway logic:
   - Year-by-year calculation
   - Inflation applied correctly
   - Income and expenses adjusted consistently

2) Confirm capital cannot silently go negative without warning.

3) Add Liquidity Ratio display:
   Liquid Capital ÷ Annual Expenses

=================================================
PART 5 – SENSITIVITY COVERAGE
=================================================

Confirm current sensitivity covers:
   - Interest rate change
   - Expense increase

Evaluate whether to add:
   - Income reduction stress
   - Temporary income shock

Only recommend if materially beneficial.

=================================================
PART 6 – PENSION & CGT TRANSPARENCY
=================================================

1) Confirm pension valuation assumption clarity.
2) Confirm whether CGT on investments is modelled.
3) If not, recommend explicit assumption wording.

=================================================
PART 7 – STABILITY SCORE AUDITABILITY
=================================================

Confirm that:
   - All deductions are traceable.
   - Breakdown is visible.
   - No subjective factors are included.

If score uses heuristic logic, formalise deduction logic explicitly.

=================================================
PART 8 – EDGE CASE RESILIENCE
=================================================

Confirm model handles:

   - Zero income
   - Zero expenses
   - Negative equity
   - 100/0 split
   - Extremely high interest rates
   - Extremely high asset values

No crashes.
No unrealistic outputs.
No divide-by-zero.

=================================================
PART 9 – OUTPUT CONSISTENCY
=================================================

Confirm:

   - UI results reconcile exactly with report.
   - Preview values reconcile with full results.
   - No rounding inconsistencies.

=================================================
PART 10 – FINAL RECOMMENDATION
=================================================

Provide:

1) Areas of weakness.
2) Areas over-engineered.
3) Additions that materially improve robustness.
4) Additions that increase liability without value.
5) What should NOT be added to keep model durable.

Be structured.
Be specific.
Assume UK regulatory context.
Assume direct-to-consumer positioning.

END REVIEW.

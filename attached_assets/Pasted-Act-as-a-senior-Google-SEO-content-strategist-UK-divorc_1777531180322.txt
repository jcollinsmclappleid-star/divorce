====================================================
SAFE CONTENT RULES
====================================================

Do not say or imply:

- what settlement someone should accept
- what split is fair
- what the court would award
- what someone is entitled to
- whether someone should keep or sell the house
- whether someone should transfer pensions
- whether someone should pay or receive maintenance
- whether a mortgage is affordable for them
- that the tool replaces a solicitor, mediator, financial adviser, pension expert, tax adviser or mortgage broker

Avoid these phrases:
- you should
- you are entitled to
- the court will
- the court is likely to
- the best option
- the fairest split
- recommended settlement
- accept this
- reject this
- suitable for you
- financial advice
- legal advice
- mortgage advice
- tax advice

Use safe language:
- may be relevant
- could be a point to discuss
- based on the figures entered
- from a cashflow perspective
- this can help you understand
- professional review may be needed
- the model illustrates
- different outcomes can create different cashflow pressures
- this guide is for general information and modelling context only

Add this safety note near the bottom of every guide:

“This guide provides general information and financial modelling context only. It is not legal, financial, tax or mortgage advice and does not recommend what either party should do.”

FAQ REQUIREMENTS
====================================================

Each guide should include or improve 4–6 FAQs.

Example FAQ style:

Q: Can a divorce calculator tell me what I will receive?
A: No. A calculator cannot decide legal fairness or predict an outcome. It can help model financial scenarios using the figures entered, including property, pensions, income, debts and cashflow.

Q: Can this replace a solicitor?
A: No. The tool is not a legal service. It helps users organise and understand the financial modelling before professional conversations.

Q: What figures do I need?
A: Useful figures include property value, mortgage balance, pensions/CETV, income, debts, savings, investments and monthly costs.

Q: Does the calculator decide the best settlement?
A: No. It compares modelled scenarios and highlights pressure points. It does not recommend what either party should do.

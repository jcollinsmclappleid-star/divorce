FULL SITE REVIEW – LEGAL RISK, LIABILITY, CONVERSION, AND REPORT STRUCTURE AUDIT

Context:
We have developed a UK-focused divorce financial modelling tool with:
- Input wizard
- Scenario modelling (Sell / Retain Property)
- Settlement sliders
- Stability score
- Source-of-funds breakdown
- Monthly snapshot
- 5-year projection
- Premium unlock model (£79, 6-month access)
- Structured Financial Brief (PDF export)

We now require a structured audit and enhancement review before public launch.

Your task:
Conduct a comprehensive review and report back in structured format under the following headings.

=================================================
PART 1 – LIABILITY & REGULATORY RISK REVIEW
=================================================

Review entire site copy, modelling outputs, disclaimers, PDF language, and positioning.

Assess risk in the following categories:

1) Financial Advice Risk
- Does any language imply personalised financial advice?
- Does any output cross from modelling into recommendation?
- Does the tool present itself as advisory rather than illustrative?

2) Legal Advice Risk
- Any language implying entitlement?
- Any language implying predicted court outcomes?
- Any statements that could be interpreted as legal guidance?

3) Tax Advice Risk
- Any calculations that could be interpreted as tax planning advice?
- Any wording that implies compliance or tax optimisation?
- Any areas where UK tax references create exposure?

4) Misrepresentation Risk
- Any claims about accuracy that are too strong?
- Any implied guarantees?
- Any unrealistic expectations set?

5) Consumer Protection / Fair Trading Risk (UK context)
- Is pricing transparent?
- Are access terms clear?
- Is 6-month access clearly defined?
- Are refund or dissatisfaction policies unclear?

Deliver:
- Risk level (Low / Medium / High) per category
- Specific examples of risky language
- Exact recommended rewording
- Any sections that should be removed entirely

=================================================
PART 2 – CONVERSION & POSITIONING REVIEW
=================================================

Evaluate:

1) Homepage clarity
- Is value proposition clear within 5 seconds?
- Is emotional framing balanced and professional?
- Does it differentiate from a free calculator?

2) Preview → Unlock flow
- Is enough value shown before paywall?
- Is too much shown?
- Where could drop-off occur?

3) Pricing psychology
- Does £79 feel justified?
- Are benefits framed as outcomes or features?
- Is 6-month unlimited access communicated clearly?

4) Unlock page structure
- Is it too long?
- Is it too dense?
- Are benefits clear?
- Is urgency subtle but present?

Deliver:
- 5 concrete conversion improvements
- 3 elements to remove if unnecessary
- 3 additional ideas that increase perceived premium value without adding regulatory risk

=================================================
PART 3 – CONTENT VALUE OPTIMISATION
=================================================

Review all information presented on:

- Results page
- Reports
- Unlock page
- Homepage

Identify:

1) Information that does not add meaningful decision value and can be removed.
2) Information that is repeated unnecessarily.
3) Missing information that would materially increase clarity.
4) Areas where explanation is too complex or too simplified.
5) Opportunities to improve structured qualitative summaries.

Be specific.
Do not give generic advice.

=================================================
PART 4 – PROFESSIONAL REPORT STRUCTURE REDESIGN
=================================================

Redesign the Structured Financial Brief (PDF + on-screen equivalent) into a format that resembles a professional financial planning document.

Provide a clearly structured format including:

1) Cover Page
- Title
- Date
- Client identifier (if applicable)
- Disclaimer

2) Executive Summary
- Scenario comparison table
- Key financial indicators
- Funding requirements
- Stability overview
- Clear qualitative summary

3) Scenario Analysis Sections (Each Scenario Separate)
For each scenario include:

- Headline Summary
- Structured Narrative Overview
- Source of Funds Table
- Net Worth Composition Breakdown
- Monthly Financial Position
- Stability Assessment
- Sensitivity Considerations
- Key Risks & Observations

4) Assumptions & Methodology
- Inputs
- Settlement ratio
- Interest rate assumptions
- Expense assumptions
- Limitations of modelling
- Formal disclaimer language

5) Appendix (if required)
- Detailed calculation breakdown
- Terminology definitions

Ensure:
- Clear hierarchy
- Professional tone
- No casual phrasing
- Proper segmentation
- Readable tables
- Defined headings

Deliver:
- Revised report outline
- Recommended headline phrasing
- Suggested narrative tone
- Formatting guidance (spacing, typography hierarchy)

=================================================
PART 5 – FINAL STRATEGIC EVALUATION
=================================================

Provide:

1) Overall commercial viability assessment.
2) Overall regulatory risk assessment.
3) Top 3 priority fixes before public launch.
4) What can be launched immediately vs what should wait.
5) Whether any features increase liability without adding conversion value.

Be structured.
Be specific.
Avoid generic advice.
Assume UK regulatory context.
Assume direct-to-consumer audience.

END OF REVIEW REQUEST.
